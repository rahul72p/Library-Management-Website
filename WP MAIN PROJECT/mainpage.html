The STUDY CENTER Library is an oasis of calm and quiet, kitted-out with wireless technology as well as newspapers, magazines and ranks of books. Recently refurbished it is an area for silent working which preserves the traditional with new modern decor. With ample sockets and data cabling, mobile bookcases and iPads to access the library catalogue it is a flexible space that is truly future-proof. Open daily, it provides a variety of different resources including classic and contemporary fiction, textbooks and background reading for subject work, reference books including dictionaries and encyclopaedia, six daily newspapers and 30 magazines, music CDs and foreign films on DVD. Biographical information, newspapers and other online databases covering curriculum information are also available. Four members of library staff are available for advice and help with finding information and teaching information literacy.



Accessed from THE STUDY CENTER Library are the Anne Cadbury Resource Room, with spaces for 30 students with laptops and a large TV for presentations; the Cadbury Room for collaborative working;he library is a place for learning and reflection, and our staff supports these activities by providing helpful, responsive, and knowledgeable service. Mutual courtesy and respect among users and staff are essential to promoting the educational mission of the library. .






Leaflets are provided to help with a variety of library-related topics including alternative search engines, referencing, citation and using the catalogues and databases as well as recommending books for leisure reading or extended reading for curriculum subjects. A number of genre leaflets are available.One of our highest goals is to create a setting where our users feel free to pursue research and study without compromising their privacy or safety. The STUDY CENTER Library is in compliance with University policies, including those outlined by Student Accountability, Community Standards, & Academic Integrity and Vanderbilt Human Resources. Our libraries contain a variety of spaces for quiet study. Please be courteous to others and aware of conversation in open areas and when using electronic devices. All conduct should be consistent with and support the research and scholarship of the STUDY CENTER community.
